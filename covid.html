Limitations of Twitter

 With the acknowledgement of the need for a more massive dataset and a more professional analyzing tool than human analysts, we noticed that Twitter users receive biased feeds based on their political opinions. If you are a supporter for one of the candidates or parties, it is harder to reach tweets that are in favor of the other. This results in consolidating people‚Äôs thoughts even more and it would be harder for anyone who wants to stay neutral or objective. It creates a world that you have to pick a side and put your root in it. However, this is an impact and it is not necessary to categorize it as a benefit or a limitation: it is beneficial for people as it is more convenient for them to obtain the information that they need, while it is harmful as it may block people‚Äôs sight at the same time.
 However, we did notice a limitation of Twitter‚Äôs search engine when we used it to gather information: there were not just two perspectives regarding this topic and the search results blocked almost all the perspectives other than democrats (Biden) and republicans (Trump). Both the presidential debate and the Covid-19 issue are global topics. Not only Americans were paying attention to them and discussing them. The hashtags #debate2020 and #election2020 were the most popular ones, but they rarely included any other tweets except those that were posted by Americans. It is true that there is a filter for languages in the search engine, but the amount of data is significantly low for studying. Except for the reason that fewer people outside the US reacted to these topics, there is also another possibility that people who are native in other languages do not use an English hashtag. Even though they may have a discussion in another hashtag that is worth to be included in the study, it is almost impossible for the mass public to know and understand their opinions.
